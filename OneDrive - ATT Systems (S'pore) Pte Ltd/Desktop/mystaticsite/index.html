The world in early 2026 is witnessing an alarming rise in armed conflicts — more than 130 active wars according to recent reports, double the number from just 15 years ago. From sudden escalations in the Middle East to grinding attritional wars in Eastern Europe and Africa, the humanitarian toll continues to mount.

The New Front: US-Israel vs Iran War (February–March 2026)

On February 28, 2026, a major escalation began when Israel and the United States launched large-scale strikes on Iranian military and nuclear sites, reportedly killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei in the opening hours. Now in its early days, the conflict has seen missile exchanges, strikes on Tehran and Beirut, and involvement of Hezbollah in Lebanon.

Russia–Ukraine: Attrition Enters Year Five

The full-scale invasion continues with slow Russian advances in the east, massive drone/missile strikes, and Ukrainian defensive operations. Daily casualties remain high, with Russia reporting hundreds of losses in single days.

Sudan Civil War: Humanitarian Catastrophe Deepens

Entering its fourth year, the power struggle between SAF and RSF has caused famine, mass displacement, and atrocities including sexual violence. Recent fighting has seen SAF gains in Khartoum but spillover risks into neighboring countries.
